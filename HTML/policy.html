Private Policy

Application, Website and Product users
Like most application and website operators, GO1 collects non-personally-identifying information of the sort that web browsers and servers typically make available, such as the browser type, language preference, referring site, and the date and time of each visitor request. GO1’s purpose in collecting non-personally identifying information is to better understand how GO1’s visitors use its website. From time to time, GO1 may release non-personally-identifying information in the aggregate, e.g., by publishing a report on trends in the usage of its website.
GO1’s applications and products have the ability to collect information on users of those platforms.  This information purely for the purposes of user authentication, and stores this information for customer reference purposes only. GO1 does not disclose any personally-identifying information to any party. 

Gathering of Personally-Identifying Information
Certain visitors to GO1’s applications or websites choose to interact with GO1 in ways that require GO1 to gather personally-identifying information. The amount and type of information that GO1 gathers depends on the nature of the interaction.  In all such cases this information is required to authenticate users as part of GO1’s standard license terms for use of a GO1 platform (either a trial or paid usage).  
Those who engage in transactions with GO1 – including but not limited to, for example only, by seeking access to the GO1 Training Portal – are asked to provide additional information, including as necessary the personal and financial information required to process those transactions. In each case, GO1 collects such information only insofar as is necessary or appropriate to fulfill the purpose of the visitor’s interaction with GO1. GO1 does not disclose personally-identifying information other than as described below. And visitors can always refuse to supply personally-identifying information, with the caveat that it may prevent them from engaging in certain website or platform related activities.

Protection of Certain Personally-Identifying Information
GO1 will not disclose potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information to any party other than to its own employees or contractors and only if that employee or subcontractor (i) needs to know that information in order to process it on GO1’s behalf or to provide services to the customer (ii) has agreed not to disclose it to others. Some of those employees or contractors may be located outside of your home country; by using GO1’s websites and platforms, you consent to the transfer of such information to them. GO1 will not rent or sell potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information to anyone. Other than to its employees as described above, GO1 discloses potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information only in response to a subpoena, court order or other governmental request, or when GO1 believes in good faith that disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect the property or rights of GO1, third parties or the public at large. If you are a registered user of a GO1 website or platform and have supplied your email address, GO1 may occasionally send you an email to tell you about new features, solicit your feedback, or just keep you up to date with what’s going on with GO1 and our products. We primarily use our various product blogs to communicate this type of information, so we expect to keep this type of email to a minimum. If you send us a request (for example via a support email or via one of our feedback mechanisms), we reserve the right to publish it in order to help us clarify or respond to your request or to help us support other users. GO1 takes all measures reasonably necessary to protect against the unauthorised access, use, alteration or destruction of potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information.
If GO1, or substantially all of its assets, were acquired, or in the unlikely event that GO1 goes out of business or enters bankruptcy, user information would be one of the assets that is transferred or acquired by a third party. You acknowledge that such transfers may occur, and that any acquirer of GO1 may continue to use your personal information as set forth in this policy.

Cookies
A cookie is a string of information that a website stores on a visitor’s computer, and that the visitor’s browser provides to the website each time the visitor returns. GO1 uses cookies to help GO1 identify and track visitors, their usage of GO1 website, and their website access preferences. GO1 visitors who do not wish to have cookies placed on their computers should set their browsers to refuse cookies before using GO1’s websites, with the drawback that certain features of GO1’s websites may not function properly without the aid of cookies.

Privacy Policy Changes
Although most changes are likely to be minor, GO1 may change its Privacy Policy from time to time, and in GO1’s sole discretion. GO1 encourages visitors to frequently check this page for any changes to its Privacy Policy.
